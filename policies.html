Policies

Infection Control Policy

Children who are ill with any infectious diseases, outlined in our Infectious Control Policy document, cannot attend White Hare Day Care. However, we would ask that you inform us of the nature of the infection so that we can make careful observations of any other child who appears unwell. Your doctor will advise when your child is no longer infectious. If you have any doubt whatsoever about whether your child should come to Day Care, please ring before you leave home so that staff may advise you.
A 48 hour withdrawal period must be observed following vomiting or diarrhoea.

Uncollected Child Policy

In the event that a child is NOT collected by an authorised adult at the end of a session/day, the setting puts into practice agreed procedures. These ensure that the child is cared for safely and is caused as little distress as possible.
White Hare Day Care obtains information regarding those who have permission to collect (authorised adult) and will begin by trying to contact the parent/carer.
If unsuccessful, staff will telephone the ‘emergency contact’ person.
The child will remain at the setting, in the care of two fully vetted workers until he or she is collected.
If after 30minutes of closure and no contact has been made, then staff will contact the Emergency Duty Team at Social Services.
Under no circumstances will staff go looking for a parent/ carer, nor will they take a child home.
A full written report of the incident will be recorded. A ‘late collection fee’ will be charged for the period of lateness.
Please ensure that you keep White Hare Day Care informed of any changes in telephone numbers and addresses.

Medication

White Hare Day Care will only administer medication that has been prescribed by a doctor and in the child’s name whom it is to be administered to.
If your child has been prescribed medication by the doctor which needs to be taken during the nursery day, you will be asked to sign a medication form during drop off.
This form authorises staff to administer the medication.
This medication form will be completed in full, and contain records of the child’s name who is receiving the medication, the name of the medication, the dosage required, the time to be given, signature or parent/carer.
Educators who administer the medication will sign that they have done so, recording the time and dosage. Two educators will always be present at the administration of medication and both will sign the medication form.
Parents will be made aware of the details on collection of the child.

Complaints Procedure

At White Hare Day Care we are committed to providing a high quality service. If the service you have received is not what you expected, we encourage you to discuss the matter with us. Where appropriate any concerns or issues should be first discussed with your child’s key worker.
The manager, who operates an open door policy, also deals with any general concerns/issues about the setting and or/practice. Such issues should be made promptly so that an investigation may proceed and nay action taken quickly.
A written record of the complaint will be made, and the appropriate action taken. The full policy is available in our policy file located in the office. Staff are expected to treat children and their parents/carers with respect, therefore they must be afforded the same courtesy.
Verbal or physical abuse of any staff will not be tolerated. The proper complaints channels must be followed..
